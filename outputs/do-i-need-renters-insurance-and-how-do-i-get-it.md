# Q: Do I need renters insurance and how do I get it?

## Do You Need Renters Insurance?

**Possibly required, always recommended.** Check your lease — many landlords now require it as a condition of renting. Some universities also require it for off-campus students.

Even if it's not required, it's one of the best-value protections you can buy: **$10–$20/month** covers belongings worth thousands of dollars.

---

## What It Covers

- **Your belongings** — laptop, phone, clothes, furniture (theft, fire, water damage)
- **Liability** — if a guest is injured in your apartment
- **Temporary housing** — if your apartment becomes uninhabitable

> ⚠️ Your landlord's insurance covers the *building only* — not anything inside your unit.

---

## How to Get It (5 Steps)

1. **Inventory your belongings** — estimate total value (a move-in video works great)
2. **Choose coverage amount** — at least $15,000–$20,000 for most students
3. **Choose a deductible** — $250–$500 is typical
4. **Get quotes** from 2–3 providers:
   - **Lemonade** — most popular with international students; sign up entirely online in minutes, ~$5/month
   - State Farm, Allstate, GEICO are also reliable options
5. **Save your proof of insurance** — your landlord may ask for it

---

## Two Things to Do After Buying

- If your lease requires it, add your landlord as an **"additional interested party"** (free, done during sign-up)
- Take a **video inventory** of your valuables and store it in Google Drive or iCloud — you'll need this if you ever file a claim
