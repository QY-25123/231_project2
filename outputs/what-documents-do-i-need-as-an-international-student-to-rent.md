# Q: What documents do I need as an international student to rent an apartment in the US?

## Documents You'll Need to Rent a U.S. Apartment

As an international student, landlords commonly ask for:

### Identity & Immigration Status
- **Passport** (primary ID)
- **Form I-20** (F-1 visa students) or **DS-2019** (J-1 visa students)
- **Form I-94** (proof of legal entry and current status)

### Proof of Financial Ability to Pay
- Bank statements
- Scholarship award letter
- Financial guarantee letter from your university or sponsor

### The Credit History Problem
Most international students have **no U.S. credit history**, which landlords use to assess reliability. You have a few options:
- **U.S.-based co-signer/guarantor** — someone who accepts legal responsibility if you don't pay
- **Larger security deposit** — some landlords accept 2–3 months' rent upfront instead of requiring a co-signer. **It's worth asking directly.**

---

> ⚠️ Every landlord is different — some are very flexible with international students, others are not. Ask upfront what they require before investing time in an application.
