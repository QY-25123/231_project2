# Q: What is the difference between a fixed-term lease and a month-to-month lease?

## Fixed-Term vs. Month-to-Month Leases

Both are covered in detail in [[concepts/lease-terms]]. Here's a quick comparison:

### Fixed-Term Lease
- Has a clear **start and end date** (e.g., August 1 – July 31)
- Both you and the landlord are locked into the terms until that date
- **You cannot leave early without penalties** — typically 1–2 months' rent, or you may owe rent until a new tenant is found
- Most common type for apartments

### Month-to-Month Lease
- **No fixed end date** — continues automatically each month
- Either party can end it with written notice, typically **30 days**
- Rent can be increased with proper notice (usually 30 days)
- Landlord can also end the tenancy with proper notice

---

### Which Is Better for International Students?

| Situation | Better Option |
|---|---|
| You know exactly how long you'll be in the U.S. | Fixed-term (often lower rent) |
| Your plans are uncertain (visa, graduation timing) | Month-to-month (more flexibility) |

⚠️ **Key warning:** If you have a fixed-term lease and your situation changes (visa issues, early graduation), breaking it can be costly. Always check the early termination clause *before* you sign.
