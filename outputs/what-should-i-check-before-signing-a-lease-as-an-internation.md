# Q: What should I check before signing a lease as an international student?

## Before Signing a Lease: International Student Checklist

### Documents to Have Ready
- Passport (primary ID)
- Form I-20 (F-1) or DS-2019 (J-1)
- Form I-94 (proof of legal entry)
- Proof of funds: bank statements, scholarship letter, or financial guarantee
- **No U.S. credit history?** You may need a U.S.-based co-signer — or offer 2–3 months' security deposit upfront as an alternative

### Key Lease Terms to Review

1. **Rent amount, due date, and late fees** — Know the grace period before penalties apply
2. **Exact lease end date** — Check if it auto-renews and what notice you must give (usually 30–60 days)
3. **Early termination policy** — Breaking the lease can cost 1–2 months' rent or more
4. **Which utilities are included** — Get a written list from the landlord
5. **Subletting rules** — Most leases prohibit it without written approval
6. **Renters insurance requirement** — Many leases now require it (costs only $10–20/month)
7. **Pet and guest policies** — Even if not relevant now, note the restrictions

### ⚠️ Important Warnings
- **Never sign a lease you haven't fully read**
- Verbal explanations from landlords don't override what's written in the contract
- If anything is unclear, ask for written clarification or bring the lease to your university's international student office before signing

### Good Questions to Ask the Landlord
- What is the early termination fee?
- Does the lease auto-renew? When must I notify you if I'm not renewing?
- Is subletting allowed?
- Which utilities do I pay separately?
