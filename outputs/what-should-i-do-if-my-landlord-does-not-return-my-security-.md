# Q: What should I do if my landlord does not return my security deposit?

## If Your Landlord Doesn't Return Your Security Deposit

Here's what to do, in order:

### Step 1: Check the Legal Deadline
Most states require landlords to return your deposit within **14–30 days** of your move-out date, along with an **itemized list of any deductions**.

> ⚠️ **Important:** If your landlord misses this deadline, they may lose the legal right to make *any* deductions — and in some states owe you **double or triple** the deposit as a penalty.

### Step 2: Send a Formal Dispute Letter
1. Write a letter referencing your **move-in photos, move-in condition report, and any other documentation**.
2. Send it **via email AND certified mail** (about $3–$5 at any U.S. Post Office — certified mail provides proof of delivery).
3. Give the landlord a clear deadline to respond, e.g., 5–7 business days.

A ready-to-use dispute letter template is in **wiki/guides/landlord-communication.md** under *"Disputing a Charge."*

### Step 3: Escalate If They Don't Respond
- **Small claims court** — handles disputes typically up to $5,000–$10,000. No lawyer required; you present your documentation directly to a judge.
- **University student legal services** — free advice for students; contact them before filing so you understand your state's specific rules.

---

## How to Strengthen Your Case

Your best evidence is documentation you (hopefully) gathered earlier:
- ✅ Move-in inspection photos/video (timestamped)
- ✅ The signed move-in condition report, emailed to your landlord on Day 1
- ✅ Move-out walkthrough video
- ✅ Rent payment receipts
- ✅ All written communications with your landlord

> ⚠️ **Your visa status does not affect your legal rights here.** You are fully entitled to pursue this regardless of immigration status.
