## What Is a Lease?

"A lease is a legally binding rental agreement with fixed terms (6, 9, 12+ months) requiring careful review."
(UAB Office of Global Engagement)

Landlord-tenant law "governs the rental of residential and commercial property. It regulates lease terms and termination, tenant screening, subleasing, repairs, and eviction procedures." Regulations originate from state statutes, local ordinances, common law, and federal legislation. Many states adopt versions of the Uniform Residential Landlord and Tenant Act.
(Cornell Law LII)

A lease is a legally binding contract between you (the tenant) and the landlord. Always read the full lease before signing. Modern legislation requires that transparent lease terms be "expressed in reasonably plain language" and not hidden in fine print or complex technical language.

## Types of Leases

### Fixed-Term Tenancy
A lease with a definite beginning and ending date. Despite the name "tenancy for years," it can last any period. At the end of the term, the tenancy either expires or converts to another type.

### Periodic Tenancy (Month-to-Month)
Month-to-month, week-to-week, or year-to-year arrangements where the lease term is determined by rent payment frequency. Either party may terminate by providing statutory notice (typically one month for month-to-month tenancies).

### Tenancy at Will
Either party can end this arrangement anytime with reasonable notice.

### Tenancy at Sufferance (Holdover)
When a tenant remains after lease expiration. The landlord may evict without notice or impose new lease terms. Multi-year leases "often convert to month-to-month tenancies at pro-rated rates if not renewed."

## Key Terms & Common Lease Elements

Lease agreements typically include:
- Party names and identifiable property details
- Start date and duration
- Renewal and non-renewal conditions
- Rent amount and payment terms (amount, due date, grace period, late fee)
- Security deposit provisions
- Default conditions and remedies
- Insurance requirements (many leases now require renters insurance)
- Maintenance responsibilities
- Termination clauses and early termination penalties

**Tenant Rights Under a Lease:**
- **Quiet Enjoyment:** Tenants lawfully possess property and any interference with their quiet enjoyment is unlawful.
- **Exclusive Possession:** Tenants have exclusive use of the unit, excluding landlords and others without tenant invitation.
- **Notice Before Entry:** Landlords must provide proper notice (typically 24 hours, daytime, advance knock) before entering, except in emergencies.

**Tenant Obligations:**
- Pay rent on time
- Maintain the property in good condition (ordinary wear and tear excepted)
- Follow lease restrictions (pet policies, guest limits, use limitations)
- Maintain renters insurance coverage when required

## Key Questions to Ask Before Signing (UAB Global Engagement)

- Monthly rent amount and due date
- Payment methods accepted
- Which utilities are included in rent
- Security deposit refundability terms
- Lease duration options (month-to-month, 6, 9, or 12 months)
- **"What will I owe if I must break my lease before the end of the agreement?"**
- Auto-renewal policies and rent increase notification requirements

## For International Students: Required Documents

Landlords typically request:
- Passport
- Form I-20 (F-1 status) or DS-2019 (J-1 status)
- Most recent Form I-94
- Proof of income or assets (bank statements, scholarship letter)
- Personal references
- Guarantor/co-signer if you lack U.S. credit history

---

## Renewal & Termination

**Automatic Conversion:** Multi-year leases often convert to month-to-month tenancies if not renewed.
**Early Termination:** Breaking leases before expiration can incur penalties or require payment of remaining term costs if landlords cannot find replacement tenants.
**Subletting:** Rights vary by jurisdiction. Check your lease — many prohibit subletting without written landlord approval.
