## Your Legal Right to Repairs

The implied warranty of habitability is "a warranty implied by law (in some states) that by leasing residential property, the landlord is promising that the property is suitable to be lived in." It "generally cannot be disclaimed," distinguishing it from other implied warranties.

Landlords must provide habitable housing compliant with local building codes. Most states recognize an implied warranty of habitability, "requiring landlords to make necessary repairs." Required services include:
- **Essential services:** Heat, hot water, and other basic utilities
- **Safety requirements:** Functioning smoke alarms, compliance with fire code provisions
- **Structural integrity:** Cracked walls, peeling paint, water leaks — courts in some jurisdictions have determined these constitute violations
- **Legal occupancy:** Valid certificates of occupancy where required by municipal law

The doctrine exists "as a protection for tenants in a less advantageous bargaining position than the landlord."

## Landlord Obligations (Texas Law Help)

Landlords must repair conditions affecting physical health and safety, including:
- Pest infestations (roaches, rats)
- Sewage and roof leaks
- Electrical hazards
- Normal wear and tear on carpeting and flooring

**Conditions for repair obligations:**
- Rent must be current at the time of notice
- Problem must be reported in writing to the rent payment location
- Issue must affect health/safety (or hot water falls below 120°F)

**When landlords may refuse repairs:**
- Tenant, family, or guests caused the damage (excluding normal wear)
- Issue involves a utility with unavailable lines

## How to Request Repairs (Texas Law Help)

1. **Take photos** of problem areas — photograph every defect clearly.
2. **Provide written notice** to your landlord (certified mail, return receipt requested — if sent certified, only one notice is legally required; otherwise send two written notices).
3. **Be specific** about what needs to be repaired.
4. **Allow reasonable time** — minimum 7 days; less for emergencies like roof leaks or no heat.
5. **Keep copies** of all notices and photos.
6. **Maintain current rent payments** — landlords may not be obligated to repair if rent is unpaid.

## Categories of Urgency

### Emergency (Contact landlord immediately, 24/7)
- Gas leak (call 911 and your gas company first; leave the building)
- No heat in winter
- Major water leak or flooding
- Complete loss of electricity
- Sewage backup
- Broken door/window lock (security issue)

### Urgent (Report within 24 hours)
- Non-working refrigerator
- Oven/stove not working
- Air conditioning failure in extreme heat
- Clogged toilet (only toilet in unit)

### Routine (Report within a few days)
- Dripping faucet
- Broken cabinet
- Burned-out light fixture
- Minor pest sighting

## Protection from Retaliation

Landlords cannot retaliate for six months following a repair request by:
- Evicting tenants
- Terminating leases
- Raising rent (unless it was a scheduled or building-wide increase)

(Texas Property Code §92.052–92.061; similar protections exist in most states)

## Remedies When Repairs Are Not Made

When landlords fail to maintain habitability standards, tenants may:

**Option 1 — Terminate the lease:** If repairs aren't completed after proper written notice, tenants may provide written termination notice, receive refunds for unused rental period, and recover security deposit minus lawful deductions.

**Option 2 — Withhold rent to pay for repairs directly:** (Varies by state; follow the legal process exactly — do not do this without legal guidance.)

**Option 3 — Sue in small claims court:** To obtain an order for repairs, rent reduction from the notice date, damages (one month's rent plus $500 minimum in Texas), court costs, and attorney's fees. Cases typically heard within 10 days; limit is $20,000 in Texas.

**Option 4 — Report code violations:** Tenants may report violations "without fear of retaliation."

## Documentation Best Practices

- Always use writing (email or tenant portal) — not verbal requests
- Attach photos or video of the problem
- Note when the issue started and its location in the unit
- Specify your availability for the maintenance team to enter
- Save every maintenance email and response received
