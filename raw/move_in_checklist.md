## Why Inspections Matter

Move-in and move-out inspections document the apartment's condition at the beginning and end of a tenancy. They "help landlords and tenants agree on the property's state to prevent disputes over security deposit deductions."

"The landlord and tenant should walk the unit together during both the move-in and move-out inspections." Both parties should document findings and keep signed copies.

Tenants have a right to request a move-in inspection, and landlords must notify tenants of that right at the start of tenancy or within 14 days after move-in (varies by state). The inspection identifies existing repair problems before the tenancy to avoid wrongful deposit deductions at move-out.

---

## How to Document

1. Walk every room with the landlord before bringing in any belongings.
2. Complete the landlord's move-in condition checklist (or create your own).
3. Take photographs and/or video of every room, including every defect — faded paint, scuffs, stains, scratches.
4. Both you and the landlord should sign and date the completed checklist.
5. Email a copy of the checklist and photos to your landlord on move-in day — this creates a timestamped record.
6. Keep a copy for yourself for the duration of your tenancy.

## Normal Wear and Tear vs. Damage

**Acceptable wear and tear (you are NOT responsible for):**
- Faded paint from sunlight
- Loose wallpaper sections
- Worn or scuffed flooring from regular use
- Tangled or broken blind strings
- Old frame cracks
- Small tack/nail holes from picture hanging

**Damage (you ARE responsible for):**
- Broken windows or torn screens
- Non-working appliances (if caused by misuse)
- Excessively scratched floors
- Carpet stains or burn marks
- Pet stains and odors
- Holes larger than picture hangers
- Excessive filth at move-out

*Note: Carpet typically lasts 3–5 years, so replacement after that period is considered normal wear.*

## Room-by-Room Checklist

### Entry / Hallway
- [ ] Door locks and deadbolt work
- [ ] Walls, ceiling, floors: note any marks, holes, stains
- [ ] Closet doors and shelving condition
- [ ] Light fixtures work

### Living Room
- [ ] Walls: marks, holes, paint condition
- [ ] Flooring: scratches, stains, carpet wear
- [ ] Windows: open/close/lock, no cracks in glass
- [ ] Window blinds: condition
- [ ] All electrical outlets work
- [ ] Ceiling fan (if present)

### Kitchen
- [ ] Stove/oven: burners ignite, oven heats, no damage
- [ ] Refrigerator: cools, freezer freezes, no odors
- [ ] Dishwasher (if present): runs, no leaking
- [ ] Faucet: no drips, adequate water pressure
- [ ] Under-sink: no moisture or mold
- [ ] Cabinets and drawers: condition, function
- [ ] Garbage disposal (if present)

### Bathroom(s)
- [ ] Toilet flushes, no running water/leaking
- [ ] Shower/tub: drains properly, no existing mold/mildew
- [ ] Caulking around tub/shower is intact
- [ ] Faucet: hot and cold water work
- [ ] Exhaust fan works
- [ ] Mirror and medicine cabinet condition

### Bedroom(s)
- [ ] Closet: doors, lighting, shelving
- [ ] Windows lock properly
- [ ] Floor/carpet condition
- [ ] Walls: marks, stains

### Utilities & Safety
- [ ] Heating: turns on and heats correctly
- [ ] Air conditioning: turns on and cools
- [ ] Smoke detector works (test with button)
- [ ] Carbon monoxide detector (if present)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher present and not expired

## Benefits of a Thorough Move-In Inspection

**For tenants:** "Allows opportunity to make repairs themselves at potentially lower cost and recover full deposit" at move-out.
**For landlords:** "Identifies needed repairs, prevents disputes, encourages tenant repairs, clarifies deposit deductions."
