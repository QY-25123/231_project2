## Written Notice Requirements

"Prior to filing a suit in court for eviction, generally the landlord must provide written notice to the tenant." Similarly, tenants are expected to provide written notice before vacating. "Rules for evictions and the eviction process are determined by state, county, and city rules" — notice periods vary significantly by jurisdiction.

Most leases require 30–60 days written advance notice of intent to vacate. Check your specific lease agreement for the required notice period. Missing this window is a common and costly mistake — leases often auto-renew if proper notice is not given.

**How to send move-out notice:**
- Send via email AND certified mail (return receipt requested) so you have proof of delivery
- Include: your name, unit number, intended move-out date, and forwarding address for deposit return
- Keep a copy of the notice and the certified mail receipt

## Timeline for Move-Out

**60 days before:**
- Review lease notice requirements
- Send written non-renewal/move-out notice
- Begin gathering cleaning supplies

**30 days before:**
- Schedule utility cancellation or transfer to your new address
- Update your mailing address with bank, university, USPS, subscriptions
- Begin using up food you don't want to move

**1–2 weeks before:**
- Deep clean oven, refrigerator, bathroom grout, baseboards
- Start packing non-essentials

**Move-out day:**
- Complete final cleaning of all rooms
- Remove ALL personal belongings (check every drawer, cabinet, closet)
- Fill small nail holes (spackling paste from any hardware store)
- Return all keys, fobs, garage openers, mailbox keys
- Take a full move-out walkthrough video
- Request a joint walkthrough with the landlord

## Move-In vs. Move-Out Inspection

"The landlord and tenant should walk the unit together during both the move-in and move-out inspections." Compare your move-out video to your move-in photos. Any condition documented in your move-in report cannot be charged to you.

Timing requirements vary by jurisdiction. Some states require inspections before tenant departure; others allow them afterward. Many states require landlords to give written notice of inspection date.

## What Tenants Are Responsible For

Tenants must pay for:
- Broken windows or torn screens
- Non-working appliances (due to misuse)
- Excessively scratched floors
- Carpet stains or burn marks
- Pet stains and odors
- Holes larger than picture hangers
- Excessive filth at move-out

Tenants are NOT responsible for:
- Faded paint from sunlight (normal wear)
- Worn carpet after 3–5 years of use
- Small nail holes from pictures
- General wear from normal use

## Move-Out Cleaning Checklist

### Kitchen
- [ ] Clean oven interior and racks
- [ ] Wipe stove burners and drip pans
- [ ] Clean refrigerator inside; remove all food
- [ ] Run dishwasher with cleaning tablet
- [ ] Wipe cabinet interiors and fronts
- [ ] Clean microwave inside/outside
- [ ] Scrub sink and faucet

### Bathrooms
- [ ] Scrub toilet bowl, tank, and base
- [ ] Clean shower/tub and grout lines
- [ ] Clean sink, faucet, and drain
- [ ] Wipe mirror and medicine cabinet
- [ ] Mop floor

### All Rooms
- [ ] Vacuum carpets or sweep/mop hard floors
- [ ] Dust ceiling fans and light fixtures
- [ ] Wipe down window sills and blinds
- [ ] Remove all nails/hooks; patch holes with spackling
- [ ] Wipe all doors and door handles

## Early Termination

If you must break the lease early: "Breaking leases before expiration can incur penalties or require payment of remaining term costs if landlords cannot find replacement tenants." Some leases specify a fixed early termination fee (often 1–2 months' rent). Written notice with your reason and move date may limit your liability.
