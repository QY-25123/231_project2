## What Is a Security Deposit?

A security deposit is "a sum of money held in trust" used in residential property leasing. It is an amount that "the landlord will hold until the end of the lease for repairs" — typically one month's rent, though it can be up to two months' rent.

Security deposits are legally owned by tenants. Some jurisdictions require landlords to provide banking information about where the deposit is held, pay annual interest on it, or submit pre-existing damage lists.

---

## Legal Framework: State-Specific Limits

The Supreme Court distinguished deposits from advance payments in *Commissioner v. Indianapolis Power & Light Co.* (1990). State limits include:

- **Connecticut:** Capped at two months' rent (one month for tenants age 62+)
- **New York:** Post-2019 reforms limit to one month's rent
- **California:** Two months' rent (unfurnished); tenants may recover 2–3× deposit if wrongfully withheld
- **Chicago, Illinois:** Landlords must pay 0.01% interest on deposits
- **Connecticut:** Interest rate of 0.49% as of January 2026

Stronger tenant protections exist in Washington DC, Alaska, Illinois, and Wisconsin compared to states like Indiana or Michigan.

## What Can Be Deducted?

**Legitimate deductions (landlord may withhold for):**
- Missing rent payments
- Damage beyond normal wear and tear

**Normal wear and tear (NOT deductible):**
- Paint touch-ups, faded paint
- Minor cleaning
- Small tack/nail holes
- Nicks and scratches from regular use
- Worn carpet from normal foot traffic

**Damage beyond normal wear (deductible):**
- Large holes in walls
- Carpet stains or burn marks
- Broken doors or windows
- Pet stains and odors
- Excessively scratched floors

Note: Carpet typically lasts 3–5 years; replacement after that period constitutes normal wear.

## How Move-In Inspections Protect Your Deposit

"The landlord and tenant should walk the unit together during both the move-in and move-out inspections." Both parties should document findings and keep signed copies.

**What to document at move-in:**
- Flooring, walls, ceilings, doors (marks, scratches, paint damage)
- Windows (cracks, operation)
- Appliances (function, existing odors)
- Bathrooms (toilet function, wall marks, existing mold)

Take photographs or videos at move-in and move-out. Both parties should sign and retain copies of the inspection report.

## Tenant Remedies If Deposit Is Wrongfully Withheld

If a security deposit is wrongfully withheld, tenants may recover:
- Statutory damages (2–3× the deposit in states like California)
- Consequential damages
- Interest
- Punitive damages (in rare cases)

Most states require landlords to return the deposit with an itemized list of deductions within 14–30 days after move-out.

## Tips for International Students

- Your deposit money is legally yours. Keep documentation of all rent payments and communications.
- Email your signed move-in condition report and photos to your landlord on Day 1 — this creates a timestamped paper trail.
- If your landlord wrongfully withholds the deposit, small claims court handles disputes up to $5,000–$20,000 depending on state (no lawyer required).
