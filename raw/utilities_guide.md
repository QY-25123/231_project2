## What Are Utilities?

Utilities are essential services required for a habitable home. Under the implied warranty of habitability, landlords must maintain "essential services: heat, hot water, and other basic utilities" as well as "safety requirements: functioning smoke alarms and compliance with fire code provisions."

You should "know what utilities (electricity, internet, cable, heat, hot water) are included in the rent and get an estimate of annual charges from the utility supplier for utilities not included." Basic utilities typically include electricity, water, gas, oil, heat, and trash; your lease should detail which you pay.

**Always ask for a written utility disclosure from your landlord before you sign.** It should list which utilities you pay, which are included, and how charges are split if there are shared meters.

## Types of Utilities

### Electricity
- Required for lights, outlets, appliances, and often heating/cooling.
- Provided by local electric utility (e.g., PG&E in Bay Area, Con Edison in NYC, ComEd in Chicago).
- Set up service 1–2 weeks before move-in by contacting the utility company directly.

### Natural Gas
- Powers gas stoves, water heaters, and furnaces.
- May be the same company as electricity or separate depending on your region.
- Higher usage in winter for heating.

### Water & Sewer
- Often included in apartment rent. If not, typically billed quarterly.

### Internet
- Not usually included in rent. Providers vary by region (Comcast/Xfinity, AT&T, Spectrum, Google Fiber).
- Allow 1–2 weeks for installation appointments.
- Watch for promotional rates that expire after 12 months.

### Trash & Recycling
- Usually handled by the building; included in rent.
- Know your building's trash room/dumpster location and pickup schedule.

## Step-by-Step Setup

1. **Read your lease** — identify which utilities you are responsible for.
2. **Ask your landlord** for the names of utility providers they use for the building.
3. **Contact providers at least 1 week before move-in** to start service in your name.
4. **What you'll need:** Name, new address, move-in date, and ID (passport accepted).
5. **Security deposits for utilities:** Some providers require a deposit (~$150–$250) if you have no U.S. credit history. This is refundable after 12 months of on-time payments.
6. **Set up autopay** to avoid late fees and service interruption.

## Tenant Protections Related to Utilities

The implied warranty of habitability means landlords must ensure essential utilities remain available. If a landlord fails to maintain required services (e.g., no heat in winter), tenants may:
- Withhold rent to pay for repairs
- Sue for damages
- Report code violations without fear of retaliation
(Cornell Law LII)

## For International Students

- If you do not have a Social Security Number (SSN), bring your passport and I-20 form. Most utility companies will open an account.
- Share costs with roommates using apps like Splitwise.
- In cold-climate cities (Chicago, Boston), winter heating bills can be significantly higher — plan your budget accordingly.
- Many utility companies offer **budget billing** that averages costs across 12 months.
