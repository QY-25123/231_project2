# Move-In Inspection Guide

Your **move-in inspection** is one of the most important things you will do as a renter. It documents the condition of your apartment **before** you move in. This record protects you from being charged for damage you did not cause when you move out.

> ⚠️ **Do not skip this step.** Without move-in documentation, you may lose part or all of your security deposit for damage that was already there before you arrived.

---

## Why Inspections Matter

Move-in and move-out inspections exist so that landlords and tenants can agree on the property's condition at the beginning and end of the tenancy. They prevent disputes over security deposit deductions.

The **landlord and tenant should walk the unit together** during the move-in inspection. Both parties document findings and keep signed copies.

In many states, landlords are legally required to notify you of your right to request a move-in inspection. Take advantage of this right.

---

## How to Do Your Move-In Inspection

Follow these steps **before you bring any belongings into the apartment**:

1. **Schedule the walkthrough with your landlord** — do it before or on move-in day, with an empty apartment
2. **Walk every room together** and fill out a condition checklist
3. **Photograph and video everything** — every wall, floor, ceiling, appliance, and any existing defect
4. **Note every defect in writing** — even small ones: scuffs, scratches, stains, marks on walls
5. **Both you and the landlord sign and date the checklist**
6. **Email your landlord** the completed checklist and photos on move-in day — this creates a timestamped record
7. **Keep your copy** safely for the entire duration of your tenancy

💡 Use your phone to take a video walkthrough of every room in one continuous recording. This is hard to dispute later because the timestamp and date are embedded in the file.

## Normal Wear and Tear vs. Damage

Understanding this difference is critical. You are **not** responsible for normal wear and tear.

### ✅ Normal Wear and Tear — NOT Your Responsibility
- Faded paint from sunlight
- Small tack or nail holes from hanging pictures
- Worn or scuffed flooring from regular use
- Loose or tangled window blind strings
- Minor scuffs on walls
- Worn carpet after 3–5 years of normal use

### 🔴 Damage — Your Responsibility
- Broken windows or torn screens
- Large holes in walls
- Carpet stains, burn marks, or pet odors
- Excessively scratched hardwood floors
- Appliances that do not work due to misuse
- Excessive filth requiring professional cleaning

💡 **Carpet rule:** Carpet typically lasts 3–5 years. If the carpet is already several years old when you move in, you should not be charged for full replacement when you leave.

## Room-by-Room Checklist

Print or save this checklist and bring it on move-in day.

### 🚪 Entry / Hallway
- [ ] Front door lock and deadbolt work properly
- [ ] Walls, ceiling, and floor — note any marks, holes, or stains
- [ ] Closet doors and shelving in good condition
- [ ] Light fixtures work

### 🛋️ Living Room
- [ ] Walls — marks, holes, paint condition
- [ ] Flooring — scratches, stains, carpet wear
- [ ] Windows — open, close, and lock; no cracks in glass
- [ ] Window blinds — working and intact
- [ ] All electrical outlets work (bring a phone charger to test)
- [ ] Ceiling fan works (if present)

### 🍳 Kitchen
- [ ] Stove and oven — burners ignite, oven heats, no existing damage
- [ ] Refrigerator — cools properly, freezer freezes, no odors
- [ ] Dishwasher (if present) — runs without leaking
- [ ] Faucet — no drips, adequate water pressure
- [ ] Under the sink — no moisture, mold, or pest evidence
- [ ] Cabinets and drawers — open/close properly
- [ ] Garbage disposal (if present) — works

### 🚿 Bathroom(s)
- [ ] Toilet flushes; no running water or leaking at base
- [ ] Shower and tub drain properly; no existing mold or mildew
- [ ] Caulking around tub and shower is intact (no gaps)
- [ ] Hot and cold water work at faucet and shower
- [ ] Exhaust fan works
- [ ] Mirror and medicine cabinet in good condition

### 🛏️ Bedroom(s)
- [ ] Windows lock properly
- [ ] Closet — doors work, lighting present, shelving intact
- [ ] Floor and carpet condition — note any stains or damage
- [ ] Walls — any existing marks or stains

### 🔌 Utilities and Safety
- [ ] Heating system — turns on and warms the room
- [ ] Air conditioning — turns on and cools the room
- [ ] Smoke detector — press the test button to confirm it works
- [ ] Carbon monoxide detector (if present) — test button works
- [ ] Fire extinguisher present and not expired (check the gauge)

## After the Inspection

- File a maintenance request for any issues that need to be repaired before or shortly after move-in → see [[guides/maintenance-requests]]
- Store your signed checklist and photos somewhere safe (email them to yourself as a backup)
- Review your [[concepts/security-deposit]] article to understand how this documentation protects your deposit

## Benefits of a Thorough Inspection

| For You | For Your Landlord |
|---|---|
| Protection from wrongful deposit deductions | Clear record of what needs to be repaired |
| Legal evidence if a dispute arises | Fewer disagreements at move-out |
| Peace of mind throughout your tenancy | Documentation that clarifies responsibilities |
