# Move-Out Preparation Guide

Moving out of a U.S. apartment requires careful planning. If you miss deadlines or skip steps, you could lose your security deposit, pay extra rent, or face legal penalties. This guide walks you through everything to do before you hand in your keys.

---

## Step 1 — Give Written Notice on Time

⚠️ **This is the single most important step.** Most leases require **30–60 days of written advance notice** before you move out. If you miss this deadline, your lease may **auto-renew** — meaning you owe another month's (or more) rent even if you have already left.

**Check your lease for the exact notice period required.**

- Send your notice by **email AND certified mail** (return receipt requested) so you have proof of delivery
- Include: your name, unit number, intended move-out date, and your forwarding address for the deposit return
- Use the ready-to-use template at [[templates/move-out-notice]]

For more on writing effective communication, see [[guides/landlord-communication]].

## Move-Out Timeline

### 60 Days Before Move-Out
- [ ] Check your lease for the exact notice period required
- [ ] Send your written move-out notice (see [[templates/move-out-notice]])
- [ ] Start gathering cleaning supplies

### 30 Days Before Move-Out
- [ ] Schedule utility cancellation or transfer to your new address
- [ ] Update your mailing address with your bank, university, USPS, and subscriptions
- [ ] Begin using up food you do not want to move

### 1–2 Weeks Before Move-Out
- [ ] Deep clean oven, refrigerator, bathroom grout, and baseboards
- [ ] Start packing non-essential items
- [ ] Locate all keys, fobs, mailbox keys, garage openers — you will need to return them all

### Move-Out Day
- [ ] Complete final cleaning of all rooms (see checklist below)
- [ ] Remove ALL personal belongings — check every drawer, cabinet, and closet
- [ ] Fill small nail holes with spackling paste (available at any hardware store for $3–$5)
- [ ] Return all keys, fobs, garage openers, and mailbox keys
- [ ] Take a full video walkthrough of every room
- [ ] Request a joint walkthrough inspection with your landlord
- [ ] Confirm your forwarding address with the landlord in writing

---

## Move-Out Cleaning Checklist

### 🍳 Kitchen
- [ ] Clean oven interior and racks (use oven cleaner if needed)
- [ ] Wipe stove burners and drip pans
- [ ] Clean inside the refrigerator; remove all food
- [ ] Run dishwasher with a cleaning tablet
- [ ] Wipe cabinet interiors and door fronts
- [ ] Clean microwave inside and outside
- [ ] Scrub the sink and faucet

### 🚿 Bathrooms
- [ ] Scrub toilet bowl, tank, and outside base
- [ ] Clean shower or tub — scrub grout lines
- [ ] Clean sink, faucet, and drain
- [ ] Wipe mirror and medicine cabinet
- [ ] Mop the floor

### 🏠 All Rooms
- [ ] Vacuum carpets or sweep and mop hard floors
- [ ] Dust ceiling fans and light fixtures
- [ ] Wipe window sills and blinds
- [ ] Remove all nails and hooks; fill holes with spackling paste
- [ ] Wipe all doors and door handles

## What You Are Responsible For vs. Not

| Your Responsibility | NOT Your Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Carpet stains, burn marks, or pet odors | Worn carpet after 3–5 years of normal use |
| Large holes in walls | Small nail holes from pictures |
| Broken windows or torn screens | Faded paint from sunlight |
| Excessive filth requiring professional cleaning | Minor scuffs from normal use |
| Damaged appliances (due to misuse) | Normal wear on flooring |

💡 **Compare your move-out condition to your move-in photos.** Any defect you documented at move-in cannot be charged to you at move-out. See [[guides/move-in]] for how to do this.

---

## The Move-Out Inspection

Request a **joint walkthrough** with your landlord before or on move-out day. Both of you should walk through every room and note the condition. This is your opportunity to address any concerns before the landlord finalizes deductions.

- Bring your move-in photos and signed inspection report
- Take your own photos and video of the final condition
- Ask for a written summary of any concerns raised during the walkthrough

After move-out, your landlord must return your security deposit (with an itemized list of any deductions) within **14–30 days**, depending on your state. For full details, see [[concepts/security-deposit]].

---

## Early Termination — Breaking Your Lease

If you must leave before your lease ends:

- Review your lease for the **early termination clause** — many specify a fee of 1–2 months' rent
- If no clause exists, you may owe rent for all remaining months until the landlord finds a new tenant
- Give written notice immediately with your reason and intended move-out date
- Some states allow early termination without penalty for specific circumstances (military deployment, domestic violence, health issues) — check your state's laws

⚠️ Never simply stop paying rent and leave. This creates serious legal and financial consequences.
