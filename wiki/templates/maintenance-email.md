# Maintenance Request Email Templates

Use these templates to communicate with your landlord about repair problems. **Always communicate in writing** — email creates a timestamped record that protects you legally.

> 💡 For guidance on when and how to submit maintenance requests, see [[guides/maintenance-requests]].
> For general communication tips, see [[guides/landlord-communication]].

---

## How to Use These Templates

1. Copy the template that fits your situation
2. Replace all text in **[square brackets]** with your real information
3. Attach photos or videos of the problem when relevant
4. Send the email and **save a copy** in your apartment folder
5. If you also submit through a tenant portal, send this email as a follow-up record

⚠️ For serious issues (no heat, major leaks, sewage backup), also send a written notice by **certified mail** from your nearest U.S. Post Office. Keep your certified mail receipt.

## Template 1 — Initial Maintenance Request

**Subject:** Maintenance Request — Unit [Your Unit Number] — [Brief Description of Problem]

---

Dear [Landlord's Name / Property Manager's Name],

I am writing to report a maintenance issue in my apartment at [your full address], Unit [number].

**Issue:** [Describe the problem clearly. Example: "The kitchen faucet has been leaking continuously since November 10, 2024. Water is dripping from the base of the faucet even when it is fully closed."]

**Location in unit:** [Example: "Kitchen sink faucet"]

**Date issue began:** [Date]

**Health or safety concern:** [If applicable — Example: "The leak is causing water to pool on the floor, which is a slip hazard." If not a safety issue, write "No immediate safety concern."]

I have attached [photos / a video] showing the issue for your reference.

I am available for maintenance to enter my unit on [list your available days and times, e.g., "weekdays between 9am and 5pm"]. Please provide 24 hours' notice before entry as required by my lease.

Please let me know when this issue will be addressed.

Thank you,
[Your Full Name]
Unit [Number]
[Phone Number]
[Email Address]

## Template 2 — Follow-Up on Unresolved Issue

Use this if your landlord has not responded or the repair has not been completed within a reasonable time (typically 7 days for routine issues; less for urgent ones).

**Subject:** FOLLOW-UP — Maintenance Request — Unit [Your Unit Number] — [Issue]

---

Dear [Landlord's Name / Property Manager's Name],

I am following up on my maintenance request dated **[date of original request]** regarding **[describe the issue]** in Unit [number] at [address].

As of today, **[today's date]**, this issue has not been resolved. [If applicable: "I have not received any response to my original request."]

This issue is affecting [my health / my safety / my ability to use the apartment] because [brief explanation if applicable].

I am requesting that this repair be completed within **[reasonable timeframe — e.g., 7 days]**. Please provide a written update on when maintenance will be scheduled.

I have saved a copy of my original request for my records and will continue to document this issue.

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

[Your Full Name]
Unit [Number]
[Phone Number]
[Email Address]

## Template 3 — Formal Written Demand (Serious or Unresolved Issue)

Use this template if multiple follow-ups have been ignored, or for a serious habitability issue such as no heat, pest infestation, or sewage problems. Send by **email AND certified mail**.

**Subject:** FORMAL WRITTEN NOTICE — Unresolved Maintenance Issue — Unit [Number]

---

Dear [Landlord's Name],

This letter constitutes formal written notice of an unresolved maintenance issue at [full address], Unit [number].

**Issue:** [Describe the problem in detail]

**Date first reported:** [Date of your first written notice]
**Date of follow-up notice(s):** [Dates of any follow-up emails]
**Current status:** This issue remains unresolved as of [today's date].

This condition [affects my health and safety / violates the implied warranty of habitability] as required under [your state]'s landlord-tenant law.

I am requesting that this repair be completed within **[7 days / as required by law in your state]**. If repairs are not completed within this time, I may exercise my legal rights, which may include [terminating the lease / reporting the violation to the local housing authority / filing in small claims court].

Please respond in writing to confirm receipt of this notice and provide a repair timeline.

Sincerely,
[Your Full Name]
Unit [Number]
[Address]
[Phone Number]
[Email Address]
[Date]

*Note: This letter is also being sent via certified mail, return receipt requested.*

## Template 4 — Disputing a Charge

Use this if your landlord charges you for something you believe is not your responsibility (for example, a condition that existed at move-in, or normal wear and tear).

**Subject:** Formal Dispute of Charge — Unit [Number]

---

Dear [Landlord's Name],

I am writing to formally dispute a charge of **$[amount]** for **[description of charge, e.g., "carpet replacement"]** applied to my account on [date].

Based on my **move-in inspection report** dated [date] and photographic documentation taken on [date], this condition was **pre-existing** / falls under **normal wear and tear** and is therefore not my financial responsibility.

[If applicable: "Specifically, I documented [describe the condition] on my move-in checklist, which was signed by [landlord's name or "the property manager"] on [date]. I have attached the relevant photos and signed checklist for your reference."]

I am requesting that this charge be reviewed and removed from my account.

If you would like to discuss this matter, I am available by email or by phone at [your number]. I would appreciate a written response within [10–14 days].

Thank you,
[Your Full Name]
Unit [Number]
[Email Address]

## Tips for All Maintenance Emails

- ✅ Always include your unit number and full address
- ✅ Always attach photos or videos when possible
- ✅ Save a copy of every email you send
- ✅ Note the date and time you sent each message
- ✅ BCC yourself or forward sent emails to a personal backup address
