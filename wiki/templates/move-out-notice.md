# Move-Out Notice Template

> Use this template to formally notify your landlord that you are leaving your apartment. Sending proper written notice protects your right to get your security deposit back and avoids extra charges.

---

## Before You Send: Quick Checklist

Work through this list **before** you write or send your notice.

- [ ] Check your lease for the required notice period (commonly **30 or 60 days**)
- [ ] Find the exact date your lease ends or the date you plan to move out
- [ ] Confirm your landlord's full name and mailing address
- [ ] Note your apartment's full address including unit number
- [ ] Decide where you want your security deposit returned (your new mailing address or a bank transfer, if offered)
- [ ] Save a copy of the notice for your own records before sending

## How to Send Your Notice

Send your notice in **two ways at the same time**. Using both methods creates a strong record that you gave proper notice.

### 1. Email (Fast Delivery + Written Proof)

- Attach the letter as a **PDF file** so the formatting stays intact
- Write a clear subject line, for example:
  `Notice of Intent to Vacate – 123 Main St Apt 4B – [Your Name]`
- Send it to every email address your landlord or property manager has given you
- **Save the sent email** — do not delete it, even after you move out
- If you receive a reply confirming they received it, save that reply too

### 2. Certified Mail (Legal Proof of Delivery)

- Print the letter and sign it by hand
- Go to any **U.S. Post Office** and ask to send it by **Certified Mail with Return Receipt**
- You will receive a green tracking card back in the mail once your landlord signs for it
- The cost is usually around **$4–8 USD**
- **Keep the tracking number, the green receipt card, and your payment receipt** together in a safe folder

> **Why both methods?** Email is fast and creates a timestamp. Certified mail creates legal proof that the letter was physically received. If there is ever a dispute about whether you gave proper notice, having both protects you.

## Important Notes for International Students

**Notice period:** Most leases require **30 days** notice, but some require 60 days. Read your lease carefully. Giving shorter notice than required can result in losing part or all of your security deposit.

**Move-out date vs. lease end date:** Your move-out date should be **on or before** your lease end date. Do not assume you can stay past the lease end date without a signed renewal or extension.

**Forwarding address:** U.S. landlords are legally required to send your security deposit (and an itemized list of any deductions) to the address you provide, usually within **14–30 days** after move-out depending on your state. If you are returning to your home country, consider using a trusted friend's U.S. address or ask your landlord whether a bank transfer is possible.

**Lease-breaking situations:** If you are moving out before your lease ends (for example, because your student visa status changed), this template alone is not enough. See [[move-out]] for guidance on breaking a lease early.
